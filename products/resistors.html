What are Resistors?

Resistors are passive electronic components that limit or regulate the flow of electrical current in electronic circuits. They are one of the most fundamental and widely used components in electronics, providing precise control over voltage and current levels.

Think of a resistor like a narrow section in a water pipe - it restricts the flow of water (current) and creates a pressure drop (voltage drop). This simple principle makes resistors essential for protecting sensitive components, setting operating points, and creating voltage dividers.
