Diodes

Semiconductor devices for rectification, protection, and switching

What are Diodes?

Diodes are semiconductor devices that allow electrical current to flow in only one direction. They act like electrical one-way valves, blocking current in the reverse direction while allowing it to pass freely in the forward direction. This fundamental property makes diodes essential for rectification, protection, and signal processing in electronic circuits.

Think of a diode like a check valve in plumbing - water can flow through in one direction but is blocked from flowing backward. Similarly, a diode allows electrical current to flow from anode to cathode but prevents it from flowing in the opposite direction, making it perfect for converting AC to DC and protecting circuits from reverse voltage.

Diode Categories

Low forward voltage drop and fast switching

  • • Fast recovery

TVS Diodes

Transient voltage suppression for protection

  • • ESD protection
  • • Fast response

Rectifier Diodes

AC to DC conversion in power supplies

  • • High current rating
  • • Low leakage

Zener Diodes

Voltage regulation and reference

  • • Precise breakdown voltage
  • • Voltage regulation

Key Parameters

Forward Voltage (Vf)

Voltage drop when conducting: 0.2V to 1.2V

Reverse Voltage (Vr)

Maximum reverse voltage before breakdown

Forward Current (If)

Maximum continuous forward current

Reverse Recovery Time

Time to switch from forward to reverse bias
