Capacitors

Energy storage solutions for modern electronics

What are Capacitors?

Capacitors are passive electronic components that store electrical energy in an electric field. They consist of two conductive plates separated by an insulating material called a dielectric. Capacitors are essential for filtering, timing, coupling, and energy storage in electronic circuits.

Think of a capacitor like a rechargeable battery that can charge and discharge very quickly. When voltage is applied, it stores energy like water filling a tank. When the circuit needs power, it releases this stored energy instantly, making it perfect for smoothing power supplies and filtering noise.
