Resistors

Precision electronic components for current control and voltage division

What are Resistors?

Resistors are passive electronic components that limit or regulate the flow of electrical current in electronic circuits. They are one of the most fundamental and widely used components in electronics, providing precise control over current and voltage levels.

Think of a resistor like a narrow pipe in a water system - just as a narrow pipe restricts water flow, a resistor restricts electrical current flow. The higher the resistance value, the more it restricts current flow.

Circuit Symbol

Standard resistor symbol

Main Functions

Current Limiting

Prevents excessive current flow to protect components

Voltage Division

Creates specific voltage levels from higher voltages

Heat Generation

Converts electrical energy to heat in heating applications

Signal Conditioning

Adjusts signal levels for proper circuit operation

Working Principle

Ohm's Law

Resistors operate based on Ohm's Law: V = I × R, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance. This fundamental relationship allows precise control of electrical parameters.

V = I × R

Voltage = Current × Resistance

Material Properties

Resistance is created by the material's opposition to electron flow. Different materials have different resistivity levels, from highly conductive metals to highly resistive ceramics and carbon compounds.

  • • Carbon film: Cost-effective, general purpose
  • • Metal film: High precision, low noise
  • • Wire wound: High power handling

Key Parameters
