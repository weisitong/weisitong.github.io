Diodes

Semiconductor devices for rectification, protection, and signal processing

What are Diodes?

Diodes are semiconductor devices that allow current to flow in only one direction. They consist of a P-N junction formed by joining P-type and N-type semiconductor materials. This unidirectional current flow property makes diodes essential for rectification, voltage regulation, and circuit protection applications.

Think of a diode like a one-way valve in a plumbing system - just as water can only flow in one direction through the valve, electrical current can only flow from anode to cathode in a diode. This makes diodes perfect for converting AC to DC and protecting circuits from reverse voltage.

Working Principle

P-N Junction

A diode is formed by joining P-type (positive) and N-type (negative) semiconductor materials. At the junction, electrons from the N-side diffuse to the P-side, creating a depletion region with an electric field that opposes further diffusion.

Forward Bias: Current flows easily

Reverse Bias: Current blocked

Diode Types

Different diode types are optimized for specific applications through variations in semiconductor materials, doping levels, and junction construction.

  • • Silicon: General purpose, 0.7V forward drop
  • • Schottky: Fast switching, low forward drop
  • • Zener: Voltage regulation, reverse breakdown
  • • TVS: Transient voltage suppression

Key Parameters
