Capacitors

Energy storage components for filtering, coupling, and timing applications

What are Capacitors?

Capacitors are passive electronic components that store electrical energy in an electric field. They consist of two conductive plates separated by an insulating material called a dielectric. Capacitors are essential for filtering, energy storage, and signal processing in electronic circuits.

Think of a capacitor like a rechargeable battery that charges and discharges very quickly. It can store electrical energy when voltage is applied and release it when needed, making it perfect for smoothing power supplies and filtering signals.

Standard capacitor symbol

Main Functions

Filtering

Energy Storage

Coupling

Transfers AC signals while blocking DC components

Timing

Creates time delays in RC circuits

Working Principle

Charge Storage

When voltage is applied across a capacitor, positive charges accumulate on one plate and negative charges on the other, creating an electric field between them. The amount of charge stored is proportional to the applied voltage.

Q = C × V

Charge = Capacitance × Voltage

Dielectric Materials

The dielectric material between the plates determines the capacitor's characteristics. Different materials provide different dielectric constants, affecting capacitance value, voltage rating, and temperature stability.

  • • Ceramic: High frequency, stable
  • • Electrolytic: High capacitance
  • • Film: Low loss, precision
  • • Tantalum: Compact, reliable
