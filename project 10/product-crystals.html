Crystals & Oscillators

Precision timing solutions for accurate frequency generation and clock signals

What are Crystals & Oscillators?

Crystals and oscillators are precision timing components that generate stable, accurate frequency signals. Crystals use the piezoelectric effect of quartz to create resonant frequencies, while oscillators are complete circuits that include the crystal plus additional electronics to generate clock signals. These components are essential for digital systems, communications, and any application requiring precise timing.

Think of a crystal like a tuning fork for electronics - just as a tuning fork vibrates at a specific musical note, a crystal vibrates at a precise electrical frequency. This makes crystals and oscillators the 'heartbeat' of electronic systems, providing the timing reference that keeps everything synchronized.

Working Principle

Piezoelectric Effect

Quartz crystals exhibit the piezoelectric effect - when mechanical stress is applied, they generate electrical voltage, and conversely, when electrical voltage is applied, they deform mechanically. This creates a natural resonant frequency that is extremely stable and precise.

Crystal Types

Different crystal cuts and configurations provide various frequency ranges, stability levels, and temperature characteristics to meet specific application requirements.

  • • AT-cut: Most common, good stability
  • • BT-cut: Low frequency applications
  • • SC-cut: Ultra-high stability
  • • Tuning fork: 32.768 kHz standard
