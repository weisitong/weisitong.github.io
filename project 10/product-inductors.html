Inductors

Magnetic energy storage components for filtering, energy conversion, and signal processing

What are Inductors?

Inductors are passive electronic components that store energy in a magnetic field when electrical current flows through them. They consist of a coil of wire, often wound around a magnetic core. Inductors oppose changes in current flow, making them essential for filtering, energy storage, and signal processing applications.

Think of an inductor like a flywheel in a mechanical system - just as a flywheel resists changes in rotational speed, an inductor resists changes in electrical current. This property makes inductors perfect for smoothing current flow and storing magnetic energy.

Working Principle

Electromagnetic Induction

When current flows through an inductor's coil, it creates a magnetic field. Any change in current causes a change in the magnetic field, which induces a voltage that opposes the current change (Lenz's Law). This self-induced voltage is proportional to the rate of current change.

V = L × (dI/dt)

Voltage = Inductance × Current Change Rate

Core Materials

The core material significantly affects the inductor's performance. Different materials provide different permeability levels, affecting inductance value, saturation current, and frequency response.

  • • Air core: Linear, no saturation
  • • Ferrite: High frequency, low loss
  • • Iron powder: High current, distributed gap
  • • Laminated: Low frequency, high power

Key Parameters

Saturation Current (A)

DC Resistance (Ω)
