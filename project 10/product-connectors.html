Connectors

Reliable interconnect solutions for electrical and mechanical connections

What are Connectors?

Connectors are electromechanical devices that join electrical circuits together. They consist of plugs (male) and receptacles (female) that can be easily connected and disconnected. Connectors provide a reliable, removable interface between different parts of an electronic system, enabling modular design, easy maintenance, and flexible system configuration.

Think of connectors like the plugs and sockets in your home - they allow you to easily connect and disconnect electrical devices while maintaining a secure, reliable connection. In electronics, connectors serve the same purpose but are designed for specific applications, from tiny micro-connectors in smartphones to heavy-duty industrial connectors.

Working Principle

Contact Physics

Connectors work by creating physical contact between conductive materials, typically metals like copper, gold, or silver. The contact force and surface finish determine the quality of the electrical connection. Good connectors maintain low contact resistance and prevent oxidation through proper material selection and plating.

Key factors for reliable connection:

• Contact force • Surface finish • Material compatibility

Connector Design

Modern connectors are engineered with precise tolerances, proper mating forces, and environmental protection. Design considerations include current carrying capacity, voltage rating, insertion/extraction forces, and environmental sealing.

  • • Pin/socket configuration
  • • Housing materials (plastic/metal)
  • • Locking mechanisms
  • • Environmental sealing

Key Parameters
