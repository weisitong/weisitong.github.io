Resistors

Precision resistors for current limiting and voltage division

What is a Resistor?

A resistor is a passive electronic component that limits or regulates the flow of electrical current in an electronic circuit. Think of it as a 'speed bump' for electricity - it slows down the current flow and converts excess electrical energy into heat.

Resistors are fundamental components in virtually every electronic device, from simple LED circuits to complex computer processors. They ensure that other components receive the correct amount of current and voltage to function properly.

Circuit Symbol

⟲

International standard resistor symbol (zigzag line)

How Resistors Work

Current Flow

Electric current flows through the resistive material

Resistance

Material opposes current flow, creating voltage drop

Heat Generation

Electrical energy converts to heat energy (Joule heating)

Ohm's Law

V = I × R
V = Voltage (Volts)
I = Current (Amperes)
R = Resistance (Ohms)

Resistor Types

Chip Resistors (SMD)

  • • Surface mount technology for compact designs
  • • Common sizes: 0201, 0402, 0603, 0805, 1206
  • • High precision and stability
  • • Automated assembly compatible

Through-hole Resistors

  • • Traditional leaded components
  • • Easy to handle and replace
  • • Better heat dissipation
  • • Suitable for prototyping

Power Resistors

  • • High power handling capability (>1W)
  • • Excellent thermal management
  • • Used in power supplies and motor drives
  • • Various mounting options

Variable Resistors

  • • Adjustable resistance value
  • • Potentiometers and trimmers
  • • Volume controls and calibration
  • • Linear and logarithmic tapers

Key Parameters

Resistance Value (Ω)

The opposition to current flow, measured in Ohms

Range: mΩ to GΩ

Tolerance (%)

Acceptable deviation from nominal resistance value

Common: ±1%, ±5%, ±10%

Power Rating (W)

Maximum power the resistor can safely dissipate

Range: 1/32W to 100W+

Temperature Coefficient

Resistance change per degree temperature change

Typical: ±50 to ±200 ppm/°C

Voltage Rating (V)

Maximum voltage that can be applied safely

Depends on size and construction

Package Size

Physical dimensions affecting power and precision

SMD: 0201 to 2512, THT: Various

Applications & Use Cases

LED Current Limiting

Protect LEDs from excessive current that could damage them

Voltage Division

Create specific voltage levels for different circuit sections

Pull-up/Pull-down

Ensure digital inputs have defined logic states

Filtering Circuits

Combined with capacitors to filter unwanted frequencies

Audio Controls

Volume controls and tone adjustments in audio equipment

Temperature Sensing

Thermistors for temperature measurement and control
