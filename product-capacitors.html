Capacitors

Energy storage components for filtering and power management

What is a Capacitor?

A capacitor is a passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field. Think of it as a tiny rechargeable battery that can charge and discharge very quickly. It consists of two conductive plates separated by an insulating material called a dielectric.

Capacitors are essential in electronic circuits for filtering noise, smoothing power supplies, coupling signals between circuit stages, and providing temporary energy storage. They're found in everything from simple flashlights to complex computer systems.

Circuit Symbol

||

Two parallel lines representing the capacitor plates

How Capacitors Work

Charging

Electrons accumulate on one plate, creating electric field

Energy Storage

Electric field stores energy between the plates

Discharging

Stored energy releases when circuit provides path

Key Formulas

Q = C × V

Charge = Capacitance × Voltage

E = ½CV²

Energy = ½ × Capacitance × Voltage²

Capacitor Types

MLCC (Multi-Layer Ceramic)

  • • High capacitance density in small packages
  • • Excellent frequency characteristics
  • • Wide temperature range operation
  • • Low ESR and ESL

Electrolytic Capacitors

  • • High capacitance values (μF to mF range)
  • • Polarized - must observe polarity
  • • Cost-effective for bulk capacitance
  • • Common in power supply filtering

Tantalum Capacitors

  • • Stable capacitance over temperature
  • • Low leakage current
  • • High reliability and long life
  • • Used in critical applications

Film Capacitors

  • • Excellent stability and low loss
  • • Self-healing properties
  • • High voltage capability
  • • Audio and precision applications

Key Parameters

Capacitance (F)

Ability to store electric charge, measured in Farads

Range: pF to mF

Voltage Rating (V)

Maximum voltage that can be safely applied

Common: 6.3V to 1000V+

ESR (Equivalent Series Resistance)

Internal resistance affecting efficiency and heating

Lower is better for most applications

Temperature Coefficient

Capacitance change with temperature variation

Types: C0G, X7R, Y5V, etc.

Tolerance (%)

Acceptable deviation from nominal capacitance

Common: ±5%, ±10%, ±20%

Leakage Current

Small current that flows through the dielectric

Important for timing and precision circuits

Applications & Use Cases

Power Supply Filtering

Smooth out voltage ripples in DC power supplies

AC Coupling

Timing Circuits

Create time delays with resistors in RC circuits

EMI Suppression

Energy Storage

Provide backup power for brief interruptions

Signal Processing
