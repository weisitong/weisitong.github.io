Crystals & Oscillators

Precision timing solutions for accurate frequency generation

What are Crystals & Oscillators?

Crystals and oscillators are precision timing components that generate stable, accurate frequencies for electronic systems. Think of them as the 'heartbeat' of electronic devices - they provide the timing reference that synchronizes all digital operations. Quartz crystals use the piezoelectric effect to vibrate at precise frequencies when electrical voltage is applied.

These components are essential in virtually every electronic device that requires timing - from simple digital clocks to complex microprocessors, communication systems, and precision measurement instruments. They ensure that data processing, communication protocols, and system operations occur at exactly the right time.

How Crystals Work

Piezoelectric Effect

Applied voltage causes quartz crystal to deform mechanically

Mechanical Resonance

Crystal vibrates at its natural resonant frequency

Electrical Output

Mechanical vibration generates stable electrical signal

Frequency Stability

Quartz crystals provide exceptional frequency stability, typically within ±20 to ±100 parts per million (ppm) over temperature and time. This precision makes them ideal for applications requiring accurate timing, such as computer clocks, communication systems, and precision instruments.

Crystal & Oscillator Types

Quartz Crystals

  • • Passive components requiring external circuitry
  • • Ultra-high frequency stability
  • • Low power consumption
  • • Wide frequency range: 32.768kHz to 200MHz+

Crystal Oscillators

  • • Complete oscillator circuits with built-in amplification
  • • Ready-to-use clock signals
  • • Various output formats (CMOS, TTL, sine wave)
  • • Enable/disable and standby functions

TCXO (Temperature Compensated)

  • • Built-in temperature compensation circuitry
  • • Superior frequency stability over temperature
  • • Typical stability: ±0.5 to ±2.5 ppm
  • • Used in precision applications

VCXO (Voltage Controlled)

  • • Frequency adjustable via control voltage
  • • Used in phase-locked loops (PLLs)
  • • Frequency modulation capability
  • • Communication and timing applications

Key Parameters

Frequency (Hz)

Fundamental oscillation frequency of the crystal

Range: 32.768kHz to several GHz

Frequency Tolerance (ppm)

Initial frequency accuracy at room temperature

Typical: ±10 to ±100 ppm

Temperature Stability

Frequency variation over operating temperature range

Standard: ±20 to ±100 ppm

Load Capacitance (pF)

External capacitance required for proper oscillation

Common: 8pF, 12pF, 18pF, 20pF

ESR (Equivalent Series Resistance)

Internal resistance affecting oscillation startup

Lower ESR enables easier startup

Aging Rate

Long-term frequency drift over time

Typical: ±1 to ±5 ppm/year
